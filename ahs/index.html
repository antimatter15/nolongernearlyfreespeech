    Electronics

    Students explore career fields in electricity and electronics. The course includes information on electrical and electronic theory and applications, computers aided instructions and circuit simulation, components, circuitry troubleshooting, use of test instruments and lab training devices, consumer information, and career opportunities. The course covers the fundamentals of basic electronics through complex A.C. and D.C. circuitry. Students interested in engineering or related careers will benefit from the course.

    Electronics 1 is taught to 9th to 12th graders during R1. Electronics 2 and 3 are taught during R3 and is available to students who have taken the previous level.

    Design and Technology

    Design and Technology is designed as a foundation high school course in industrial technology. Students use core technology knowledge to solve open-ended problems and create systems. Laboratory activities engage students in the design process through projects that integrate math, science, and other core subjects. Working in groups, students build and control systems in the development of technology.

    For the 2011-2012 school year, Mr. Harris teaches this class during the W8 period.

    Basic Technical Drawing

    With the introductory Technical Drawing class, students learn the basics of technical drawing. They build and design projects from specifications and the principle of IDEATE (Identify the problem, Design a solution, Experiment with approaches, Analyze potential approaches, Test implmentation, Evaluate the solution).

    For the 2011-2012 school year, Mr. Harris teaches the class during the W2 period.

    engineering drawing

    Engineering Drawing

    Students gain an understanding of technical drawing careers in modern industry. The course includes engineering-drafting problems, use of computer assisted drafting (CAD) equipment, and drafting skills and techniques. Students use handbook standard and specifications along with other resource materials. The students interested in engineering or other related careers will benefit from this course. Students competing Engineering Drawing and Basic Technical Drawing with a grade of B or better may be eligible to reticulate three credits at the Northern Virginia Community College. In addition, if they complete Art 1 and Computer Applications with a grade of B or better they maybe eligible to reticulate up to eight additional credits at the Northern Virginia Community Collage.

    Technology Student Association

    The Annandale Chapter of the Technology Student Association enables students to further their interests in technology through engaging and interesting projects. Through regional, state and national events students can meet new people from other schools and interact with teammates to accomplish a common goal. TSA gives students valuable life experiences and even the opportunity of travel.
    botball

    Botball

    Annandale competes in Botball, a robotics competition which involves the building of a robot over an 8 week period to solve some metaphorical problem which reflects some real world current situation which could be solved with technical ingenuity. After the robot is built and tested and the documentation is written and reviewed, students go to compete against other teams across the region.
